About the Temple

The sacred Sri Istakameswara Swamy Temple at Gurazala, Palnadu District, Andhra Pradesh, was constructed in the 12th century by Nayakuralu Nagamma, a great minister of the Palnadu Kingdom.

Devotees believe the temple is watched over by Naga Devata, a divine serpent said to circle the shrine at night. There are also tales of a mystic cave path linking this temple to the temples of Macherla Chennakesava Swamy and Karampudi Lord Shiva.

This ancient shrine stands at Burel Ground, radiating peace and power since centuries.
