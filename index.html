About the Temple

The sacred Sri Istakameswara Swamy Temple at Gurazala, Palnadu District, Andhra Pradesh, is a divine abode of Lord Shiva. The temple is believed to have been constructed in the 12th century by the great minister Nayakuralu Nagamma of the Palnadu Kingdom.

It is said that the temple is blessed with the divine presence of Naga Devata (Snake God) performing pradakshina around the temple at night ‚Äî a mystical belief held by the devotees.

Ancient stories tell that a hidden underground passage connects this temple to Macherla Chennakesava Swamy Temple and Karampudi Temple.

This temple stands at Burel Ground, surrounded by serenity and spiritual energy.

About Lord Shiva (Istakameswara Swamy)

  • Known as the destroyer of evil and the transformer among the Holy Trinity (Trimurti).
  • Symbolizes meditation, purity, truth, and self-realization.
  • Often worshiped in the form of Lingam representing infinite energy.
  • His divine consort is Goddess Parvati, and their son is Lord Ganesha and Kartikeya.
  • His sacred symbols include the Trishul (trident), Damaru (drum), crescent moon, and Ganga flowing from his hair.
